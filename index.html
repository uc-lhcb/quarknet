About the UC QuarkNet Summer Internship Program

Embark on a journey of scientific exploration with the University of Cincinnati's QuarkNet Summer Internship Program! This program provides high school students with an immersive learning experience in the captivating realm of particle physics. Through hands-on activities and engaging workshops, you'll delve into the world of subatomic particles, learn about some of the mysteries of their interactions and properties. Learn how scientists conduct research using advanced technologies, analyze data to uncover hidden patterns, and gain valuable insights into the fundamental physics.

Program Highlights

  • 6-week paid internship working on a research project with the UC Physics Department
  • Analyze data recorded by the LHCb experiment at CERN
  • Learn data analysis and elements of experimental particle physics
  • No prior programming experience or physics coursework required
  • Present your work at the end of the program

About LHCb and CERN

The Large Hadron Collider beauty (LHCb) experiment is one of the four main experiments at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) at CERN. It is designed to study the slight differences between matter and antimatter by investigating a type of particle called the "beauty quark" or "b quark".

CERN, the European Organization for Nuclear Research, is the world's largest particle physics laboratory. It is home to the LHC, the most powerful particle accelerator in the world, which enables scientists to explore the fundamental building blocks of matter and the forces that govern them.

As an intern in this program, you will have the unique opportunity to analyze real data from the LHCb experiment and contribute to the cutting-edge research conducted at CERN. You will gain valuable experience in data analysis, particle physics, and scientific collaboration while working alongside physicists from UC.
