About QuarkNet

QuarkNet is a national program that aims to inspire and train the next generation of particle physicists. It is a collaboration between high school teachers and students, university professors, and particle physicists from national laboratories and universities.

The main goals of the QuarkNet program are:

  • To provide high school students with authentic research experiences in particle physics
  • To foster a deeper understanding of the fundamental properties of matter and energy
  • To encourage students to pursue careers in science, technology, engineering, and mathematics (STEM)
  • To promote the exchange of ideas and resources between the education and research communities

Through the QuarkNet program, students have the opportunity to work on real particle physics experiments, analyze data, and contribute to ongoing research projects. They also attend workshops, summer institutes, and virtual learning sessions to enhance their knowledge and skills in particle physics.

The QuarkNet University of Cincinnati Summer Internship is a prime example of the program's commitment to providing hands-on research experiences for high school students. By participating in this internship, you will become a part of the vibrant QuarkNet community and contribute to the advancement of particle physics research.

About LHCb and CERN

The Large Hadron Collider beauty (LHCb) experiment is one of the four main experiments at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) at CERN. It is designed to study the slight differences between matter and antimatter by investigating a type of particle called the "beauty quark" or "b quark".

CERN, the European Organization for Nuclear Research, is the world's largest particle physics laboratory. It is home to the LHC, the most powerful particle accelerator in the world, which enables scientists to explore the fundamental building blocks of matter and the forces that govern them.

As an intern in this program, you will have the unique opportunity to analyze real data from the LHCb experiment and contribute to the cutting-edge research conducted at CERN. You will gain valuable experience in data analysis, particle physics, and scientific collaboration while working alongside physicists from UC.
